About Me

I'm a computational neuroscience PhD student at Columbia University working on models of cognition, causal inference, and reinforcement learning in psychiatry. I'm especially interested in how anticipatory value, causal beliefs, and latent structure interact to shape behavior and treatment outcomes.

I've conducted research across institutions including Columbia, NYSPI, and Princeton, with work spanning delusions, dopamine, learning, and computational psychiatry.

I'm very passionate about teaching, having taught and TA'ed several classes at Columbia and Barnard.

🦕 Outside the lab, I'm a museum enthusiast with a particular weakness for dinosaur exhibits. I also minored in theater and try to keep that creative side alive — whether it's on stage or just giving very dramatic chalk talks.
